Why It Matters for Vanilla

Humidity is the invisible lifeline of vanilla cultivation. At 72% RH, the air holds enough moisture to satisfy the plant's aerial roots (yes, vanilla has roots that absorb water and nutrients from humid air!), while remaining dry enough to prevent fungal diseases that plague tropical orchids.

The Balance

Below 60% RH, the plant desiccatesâ€”a death sentence in a greenhouse. Above 90% RH, fungi take over. The 65â€“85% window is where vanilla orchids achieve the perfect balance of hydration and disease resistance.

Growth Signal You're Watching

What Is VPD? (The Science Behind the Number)

VPD is the "comfort zone" metric. It measures the difference between the amount of moisture the air can hold (at current temperature) and the amount it actually holds (current humidity). Think of it as the "thirst" of the air.

Why It Matters for Vanilla

VPD directly controls how aggressively a plant transpires (releases water through leaves and aerial roots).

  • VPD too low (< 0.6 kPa): The air is so humid that the plant can't release water. Roots suffocate. Fungi thrive.
  • VPD too high (> 2.0 kPa): The air is so dry that the plant desperately transpires to cool itself. The plant loses water faster than roots can absorb itâ€”inducing drought stress.
  • VPD in the sweet spot (0.4â€“0.6 kPa): The plant transpires at the perfect rate. Nutrients move through the plant efficiently. Growth is maximized. The plant is neither drowning nor gasping.

Growth Signal You're Watching

When VPD is 1.2 kPa, the plant is in "active nutrient uptake mode." The transpiration rate is optimal for moving water and dissolved nutrients from the roots to leaves and flowers. This is when the magic happensâ€”when vanilla accumulates the sugars and compounds that will eventually become the vanilla flavor we prize.

Why Investors Should Care

VPD is the proxy for crop efficiency. At 1.2 kPa, our energy input (fans, misters, heaters) achieves maximum plant output. This is the data point that proves our climate control is not just preciseâ€”it's economical.

What We Control

  • Temperature-humidity coordination (maintaining ideal VPD even as outdoor conditions swing)
  • Active transpiration monitoring (our sensors trigger interventions before stress occurs)
  • Seasonal VPD targets (adjusted for different growth phases)
