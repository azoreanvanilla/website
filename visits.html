Visit Our Research Farm

Discover the secrets of vanilla—the world's most labor-intensive spice. Our greenhouse is currently undergoing renovation to create the perfect environment for learning and exploration. Join us for an unforgettable experience combining science, history, and the delicate art of vanilla cultivation.

Azores Plantations: Pineapple & Vanilla Orchids

Pineapple plantations are one of the most iconic plantation visits in the Azores. Our project offers a complementary experience: vanilla orchids cultivated in a greenhouse, where visitors can learn about pollination, climate control, and precision agriculture.

  • If you already visited a pineapple plantation, this is a great “next step” plantation tour.
  • You’ll see orchid flowers, hand‑pollination, and the science behind producing vanilla.
  • You can explore our real monitoring data on the Research Dashboard.

The Vanilla Experience

Vanilla is not just a flavor—it is a story of patience, precision, and nature's mysteries. Most of the world's vanilla comes from Madagascar, but few know the intricate process behind its production. In our greenhouse, you will witness firsthand:

  • The delicate orchid flowers that bloom for only a few hours each year
  • The ancient hand-pollination technique that unlocks the spice's potential
  • The scientific precision required to cultivate vanilla in a temperate climate
  • How the Azores' unique terroir creates a distinct vanilla flavor

Education First

Our tours are designed for curious minds—whether you're a culinary enthusiast, a science explorer, or someone fascinated by sustainable agriculture. Each visit combines interactive demonstrations, hands-on learning, and guided discussion:

  • The Science: Understand the biology of vanilla's pollination, the role of IoT sensors, and precision agriculture in the modern era
  • The Art: Witness the skill and intuition required for hand pollination and post-harvest curing
  • The Story: Learn how vanilla became valuable, its role in global trade, and why it's harder to grow than gold
  • The Future: Explore how AgTech and sustainability are reshaping specialty agriculture

FAQ

Is vanilla an orchid?

Yes. Vanilla comes from orchids (Orchidaceae). The spice is produced from vanilla orchid pods after pollination and curing.

How is this different from Azores pineapple plantations?

Pineapple plantations are an iconic Azorean tradition. Our visit is a complementary experience focused on vanilla orchids in a controlled greenhouse—pollination biology, climate control, and monitoring data.

When will tours open?
